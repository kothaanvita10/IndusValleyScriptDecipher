The Indus Valley Civilization (c. 2600‚Äì1900 BCE) was one of the world‚Äôs earliest urban civilizations. Key Challenge Areas

Undeciphered Script

The biggest challenge is that the Indus Valley script has not been decoded, limiting our understanding of their language and society.

Limited Written Records

No long texts or records have been found, making it difficult to learn about their government and beliefs.

Unclear Decline

Historians are still unsure why the civilization declined, with theories including climate change and river shifts.

Problems in deciphering the Indus Valley Script

The Indus Valley Script is one of history‚Äôs greatest unsolved problems. Because it has not been deciphered, we lack direct knowledge about how the people communicated, governed, or recorded history.

  • Short inscriptions with no long texts
  • Unknown language family
  • No bilingual translations found
  • The symbols appear on seals, pottery, and tools, but they are short, symbolic, and lack a known translation

Our Solution: Using Artificial Intelligence

Our team proposes using Artificial Intelligence (AI) and computer vision to help analyze the Indus Valley Script. AI can process thousands of symbols faster than humans and detect patterns that may not be obvious.

  • AI can compare symbol shapes and patterns
  • It can calculate similarity percentages between symbols
  • It can group symbols that may represent the same sound or meaning
  • It can analyze symbol frequency and positioning

Impact and Future Benefits

Using AI to study the Indus Valley Script could have a major impact on archaeology and history. Our solution does not replace historians ‚Äî it supports them with powerful tools.

  • Helps decode one of the world‚Äôs greatest ancient mysteries
  • Saves researchers time by analyzing thousands of symbols quickly
  • Encourages collaboration between historians and computer scientists
  • Could reveal information about Indus Valley language, trade, and culture
  • Inspires students to use technology to solve historical problems

Future Work: Real AI Model

Our current system uses computer vision techniques to analyze images. In the future, this project could be expanded using a true artificial intelligence (AI) model.

  • Machine Learning Training: A neural network could be trained on thousands of Indus Valley symbol images to recognize symbols automatically.
  • Symbol Classification: The AI could group similar symbols together and assign IDs to repeated characters.
  • Sequence Analysis: AI could analyze symbol order to detect possible words, grammar patterns, or writing direction.
  • Cross-Site Comparison: Images from different archaeological sites could be compared to study regional variations.
  • Human-AI Collaboration: Historians would still make final decisions, using AI results as supporting evidence.

This future AI system would help researchers process large amounts of data faster, while keeping humans in control of interpretation and historical meaning.
